## PROGRAM 1 — NHIS: NATIONAL HEALTH INSURANCE SCHEME (main pillar) ⭐

- **Program name:** National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS)
- **Managing body:** NHIA — National Health Insurance Authority
- **Who it is for:** all residents of Ghana. Two groups:
  - **Informal group** → pays an annual premium.
  - **Exempt group** → pays **no premium**: children under 18, persons **70 and above**, pregnant women, **indigents**, **SSNIT contributors and SSNIT pensioners**, and **LEAP beneficiaries**.
- **What is covered (benefits package):**
  - Covers **~95% of the diseases** affecting Ghanaians.
  - Outpatient (OPD) and inpatient services, dental care, emergency care, maternity care.
  - **All drugs on the NHIS approved medicines list**.
  - **Free maternal care** (see Program 2) and **free dialysis** (see Program 3) are included.
- **Cost (official NHIA tariff):**
  - Informal group: annual premium between **GH¢7 (minimum) and GH¢42 (maximum)**, based on ability to pay, **+ a card processing fee of GH¢6.00**. (Some district/private sources quote ~GH¢30–100 all-in; the statutory premium is GH¢7–42.)
  - Exempt categories: **free** (premium waived; the GH¢6 processing fee may still apply).
- **Documents required:**
  - **Adults:** **Ghana Card (National ID)** — since 2022 NHIS is integrated with the Ghana Card and it is the prerequisite for registration; passport **or SSNIT number** also accepted.
  - **Children:** birth certificate **or** the parents' NHIS card.
  - **Proof of exemption** if applicable — e.g. a pregnant woman presents an antenatal card, a signed note from a doctor/midwife, an endorsed pregnancy test, or an ultrasound report.
- **Waiting period:** normally a **one-month waiting period** applies to new members and to those renewing more than 3 months after expiry. (NHIA **temporarily waived** this from Nov 2025 until 14 Dec 2025 during "NHIS Active Month" — check whether any current waiver applies.)
- **Exact procedure (steps):**
  1. Locate the **nearest NHIS district office** (NHIS operates in all districts) **or** use the NHIS mobile app.
  2. Bring the required documents (+ proof of exemption if relevant).
  3. Register / pay the premium (if informal group).
  4. Receive / activate the NHIS membership card.
  5. **Renew every 12 months** — now possible by mobile via USSD **\*929#** or the **MyNHIS App** (select "Renew", pay with Mobile Money: MTN, Telecel/Vodafone, AirtelTigo), or at any district office.
- **Contact:** NHIA — **nhis.gov.gh** | USSD renewal: **\*929#** | **MyNHIS App** | nearest NHIS district office.
- **What it does not cover / limits:**
  - The ~5% of conditions outside the package (e.g. some specialized/expensive treatments historically excluded).
  - Informal members must keep membership **active by renewing** (lapsed cards lose benefits).
  - Coverage applies at **NHIS-credentialed facilities**.
- **Sources:** nhis.gov.gh/benefits, NHIA, Wikipedia (NHIS Ghana), socialprotection.org, Accra Street Journal (2025), Pulse Ghana (2026).

## PROGRAM 2 — FREE MATERNAL HEALTH CARE (under NHIS)

- **Name:** Free Maternal Health Care Policy (NHIS maternal benefit package)
- **Body:** NHIA (added to exemptions in 2008)
- **Who it is for:** **all pregnant women** in Ghana (and their newborn).
- **What it offers (full maternal package):**
  - **6 free antenatal visits**.
  - Emergency outpatient visits.
  - **Free facility delivery**, including all **complicated deliveries** (e.g. cesarean).
  - **2 postnatal visits** within 6 weeks.
  - **Premium and registration fees waived**.
  - Covers the **woman for 1 year** and the **newborn for the first 3 months** of life.
- **Documents required:** proof of pregnancy (antenatal card, signed note from a doctor/midwife, endorsed pregnancy test, or ultrasound report); Ghana Card if available.
- **Procedure:** register for free at the **nearest NHIS district office** as a pregnant woman with proof of pregnancy → access maternal services at NHIS facilities.
- **Contact:** NHIA — nhis.gov.gh | nearest NHIS district office / public maternity facility.
- **Limits:** some out-of-pocket costs can still occur in practice (informal charges, items not on the list); coverage of the newborn is limited to the first 3 months unless separately registered.
- **Sources:** nhis.gov.gh, NHIA, BMC/PLOS One/PMC studies on Ghana free maternal policy.

## PROGRAM 3 — FREE DIALYSIS (under NHIS)

- **Name:** NHIS Free Dialysis Programme
- **Body:** NHIA
- **Who it is for:** **kidney patients enrolled in the NHIS**. (Originally a 2024 pilot for under-18s and over-60s; since **1 December 2024** expanded to **all NHIS members** with kidney disease.)
- **What it offers:** **free dialysis sessions** (the pilot provided 8 free sessions/month) at designated hospitals; now part of the NHIS benefits package.
- **Coverage:** rolled out at **20 hospitals** across Ghana (expanding nationwide). Government funds ~GHC 57 million/year.
- **Documents required:** active **NHIS membership**; nephrologist's diagnosis/referral for kidney disease.
- **Procedure:** be an active NHIS member → obtain a kidney-disease diagnosis/referral → access free dialysis at a designated NHIS dialysis hospital.
- **Contact:** NHIA — nhis.gov.gh | designated dialysis hospital.
- **Limits:** delivered at **designated hospitals** (capacity/location constraints); continues "until the Ghana Medical Care Trust Fund becomes fully operational"; requires active NHIS membership.
- **Sources:** nhis.gov.gh/News, NHIA, MyJoyOnline, Citi Newsroom (2025), GhanaWeb.

## PROGRAM 4 — LEAP: LIVELIHOOD EMPOWERMENT AGAINST POVERTY (cash transfer)

- **Name:** LEAP — Livelihood Empowerment Against Poverty
- **Body:** LEAP Management Secretariat, **Ministry of Gender, Children and Social Protection (MoGCSP)** / Department of Social Welfare
- **Who it is for (extremely poor households with at least one of):**
  - Person **aged 65+** without any support.
  - **Severely disabled** person without productive capacity.
  - **Orphaned and Vulnerable Children (OVC)**.
  - **Pregnant women / mothers with an infant** under 1 year (added 2015).
- **What it offers:**
  - **Bi-monthly cash transfer**, amount by number of eligible members:
    - 1 member: **GH¢256** · 2 members: **GH¢304** · 3 members: **GH¢352** · 4+ members: **GH¢424** (per bi-monthly cycle).
  - **Free NHIS enrolment** (premium fee waiver) for beneficiary households.
  - Reaches ~360,000 households (as of 2024).
- **Documents required:** household identification; eligibility confirmed via a **Proxy Means Test (PMT)** during community data collection.
- **Procedure:**
  1. Communities are **selected and sensitized** about LEAP.
  2. Households go to designated points for **data collection (PMT questionnaire)** by trained enumerators.
  3. Extremely poor/vulnerable households meeting a category are selected.
  4. To inquire/apply: approach the **Department of Social Welfare** or the LEAP Secretariat.
- **Contact:** MoGCSP — **mogcsp.gov.gh** | LEAP — **leap.mogcsp.gov.gh** | District Department of Social Welfare.
- **Limits:** targeting via PMT in selected communities (not an open nationwide application); tied to the household and to eligible categories; payments follow bi-monthly cycles.
- **Sources:** leap.mogcsp.gov.gh (eligibility/payments), mogcsp.gov.gh, UNICEF Ghana, socialprotection.org, Graphic Online, GNA.

## GUARDRAIL REMINDERS (to apply in every AI response)
- Always say "you **could** be eligible", never "you are eligible".
- Always end with "**verify with the official body** before taking any steps".
- LEAP beneficiaries are selected via **PMT in selected communities** — direct the user to the **District Department of Social Welfare**, not to a free open application.
- NHIS exemption categories must bring **proof of exemption** (age, pregnancy, SSNIT, LEAP, indigent status).
- Remind informal-group members to **renew annually** (via \*929# or a district office) to keep benefits active.
- Free dialysis and free maternal care both **require active NHIS membership** — first step is often NHIS registration.
