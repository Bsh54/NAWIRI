# REQUIRED DOCUMENTS — GHANA
### NAWIRI — Exact pieces to provide for each program (the real friction point)
**Country:** Republic of Ghana
**Last updated:** 15 June 2026
**Purpose:** People miss aid most often because of ONE missing paper. This file lists, per program, the exact documents to bring AND how to obtain the foundational ones when they are missing. Sources at the bottom.

> HOW TO USE THIS (for the AI): when you point a user to a program, tell them the exact documents to prepare. The Ghana Card is the master key — if a user lacks it, getting it is usually the FIRST concrete step.

---

## SECTION A — FOUNDATIONAL / CROSS-CUTTING DOCUMENTS (get these first)

### A1. Ghana Card (National ID — NIA)
- **Why it matters:** since 2022 the NHIS is **integrated with the Ghana Card**; it is the prerequisite for NHIS registration and the master ID for Free Primary Healthcare and most services.
- **Documents to provide (one of these breeder documents):**
  - a **Birth Certificate** (original + photocopy) issued by the Births & Deaths Registry, **OR**
  - a **valid Ghana passport**, **OR**
  - a **certificate of acquired citizenship** (naturalisation/registration), **OR**
  - if you genuinely have **none** of the above: the **vouching / Oath of Identity** process, where a **relative already registered vouches for you**.
- **How / cost:** at any **NIA district office**; **first-time registration is free**.

### A2. Birth certificate (Births & Deaths Registry)
- **Why it matters:** the most common breeder document for the Ghana Card, and the document for **registering children under 18** on NHIS.
- **If lost:** a **certified copy** from the Births & Deaths Registry is accepted.

### A3. SSNIT number (for formal workers / pensioners)
- **Why it matters:** **SSNIT contributors and SSNIT pensioners are NHIS-exempt (free)**; the SSNIT number is also accepted as an adult NHIS registration document.

## SECTION B — DOCUMENTS PER PROGRAM

### Program 1 — NHIS (National Health Insurance Scheme)
- **Adults:** **Ghana Card** (A1) — the prerequisite; a **passport or SSNIT number** is also accepted.
- **Children (under 18):** **birth certificate** OR the **parents' NHIS card**.
- **Proof of exemption** (to register free) depending on category:
  - **Pregnant women:** antenatal card, a **signed note from a doctor/midwife**, an **endorsed pregnancy test**, or an **ultrasound report**.
  - **70+:** Ghana Card (age on the card is the proof).
  - **SSNIT contributor / pensioner:** SSNIT number/proof.
  - **LEAP beneficiary:** LEAP beneficiary proof.
  - **Indigent:** assessment as indigent.
- **Fees:** informal group pays the **GH¢7–42 premium + GH¢6 card fee**; exempt categories pay no premium (the GH¢6 card fee may still apply).
- **Renewal:** every 12 months via **\*929#** / **MyNHIS App** (pay by Mobile Money) or a district office.

### Program 2 — Free Maternal Health Care (under NHIS)
- **Proof of pregnancy:** antenatal card, signed note from a doctor/midwife, endorsed pregnancy test, or ultrasound report.
- **Ghana Card** if available.
- Register **free** at the nearest NHIS district office as a pregnant woman; **premium and registration fees are waived**.

### Program 3 — Free Dialysis (under NHIS)
- **Active NHIS membership** (so Program 1's documents come first).
- A **nephrologist's diagnosis / referral** for kidney disease.
- Access at a **designated NHIS dialysis hospital** (limited number of centres).

### Program 4 — LEAP (cash transfer)
- **No open application that guarantees entry** — households are **selected via a PMT survey** in communities chosen for LEAP. But to inquire/apply at the **District Department of Social Welfare**, bring:
  - **National ID (Ghana Card)**,
  - **2 passport photographs**,
  - a **digital address (Ghana Post GPS)**,
  - photocopies **and** originals of all documents.
- Eligibility category must be met: **65+ without support, severely disabled without productive capacity, OVC, or pregnant woman / mother with an infant under 1** (LEAP 1000 window).
- Selected households also get **free NHIS enrolment**.

### Program 5 — Free Primary Healthcare (FPHC) — new 2026
- **Proof of Ghanaian citizenship or legal residency** — **Ghana Card** or any valid ID showing community residency.
- Go to a **designated primary facility (CHPS compound / health centre / polyclinic)** in your district; basic care and screening are free on the spot.
- ⚠️ Phase 1 = **150 districts** only — check coverage; keep NHIS active for anything referred beyond primary care.

### Program 6 — Ghana Medical Trust Fund ("MahamaCares") — new 2025-2026
- **Active NHIS membership** (Program 1 first).
- A **medical diagnosis / referral** for a qualifying chronic NCD (cancer, hypertension, diabetes, stroke, kidney failure/dialysis).
- ⚠️ Newly operational — exact covered procedures/centres are still being detailed; advise verifying current scope with the NHIA.

## SECTION C — QUICK DOCUMENT CHECKLIST (for the AI to scan)

| If the user needs… | Make sure they have… | If missing, first step |
|---|---|---|
| NHIS coverage (adult) | Ghana Card | Register the Ghana Card free at an NIA district office (birth cert/passport, or vouching) |
| NHIS for a child < 18 | Birth certificate OR parents' NHIS card | Get/replace the birth certificate at the Births & Deaths Registry |
| Free maternal care | Proof of pregnancy | Get an antenatal card / midwife's note, then register at NHIS |
| Free dialysis | Active NHIS + nephrologist referral | Register/renew NHIS → get the kidney diagnosis/referral |
| LEAP | Ghana Card, 2 photos, digital address | Visit the District Department of Social Welfare; await PMT survey |
| Free Primary Healthcare | Ghana Card / proof of residency | Check your district is in Phase 1 (150 districts); go to a CHPS/health centre |
| Anyone with NO breeder document | — | Use NIA **vouching / Oath of Identity** (a registered relative vouches) |
