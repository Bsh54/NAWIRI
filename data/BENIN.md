> ⚠️ IMPORTANT METHODOLOGICAL NOTE — HOW BENIN'S HEALTH INSURANCE ACTUALLY WORKS (verified June 2026)
> The system has evolved in three legal layers — get this right, it is the #1 credibility point:
> 1. **RAMU** — Law 2015-42 (17 March 2016): the founding **legal framework** for universal health insurance. Still the legal reference, but not an operational service desk.
> 2. **ARCH** (Assurance pour le Renforcement du Capital Humain): the **government project** that operationalizes social protection, run by the **ANPS** (National Social Protection Agency). ARCH delivers the **free health insurance for the poor** plus training, credit and pension components.
> 3. **AMO — Assurance Maladie Obligatoire** (Mandatory Health Insurance): **Decree 2023-327 of 21 June 2023**, then **Decree 2025-672 of 29 October 2025** which makes coverage **truly mandatory for everyone**. Employers must cover all employees by **29 October 2026** (penalty: 200,000 FCFA per uncovered employee). The poor are covered by the State (via ARCH/ANPS); workers/employers contribute.
> → **The correct entry point today = ANPS** (not "ANAM / ramubenin.com" as stated in NAWIRI.md — that contact is outdated). One can also subscribe through an **approved private insurer**.

## LEGAL REFERENCE FRAMEWORK — RAMU (context, not a service desk)

- **Official name:** Universal Health Insurance Scheme (RAMU — Régime d'Assurance Maladie Universelle)
- **Legal basis:** Law No. 2015-42 of 17 March 2016 (adopted 28 December 2015), promulgated by decree. Local source file: `loi-2015-42.pdf`.
- **Nature:** **mandatory** scheme with progressive rollout (Art. 3).
- **What RAMU legally covers (Art. 24, 26):** preventive, curative and rehabilitative care, required medication, maternity — including: consultations and hospitalization; medication, medical consumables and labile blood products; laboratory tests and radiology; surgical procedures; physical rehabilitation/functional readaptation; mandatory vaccines; locally manufactured orthopedic devices; medical referral transport; pregnancy- and childbirth-related care.
- **Legal exclusions (Art. 29):** comfort care, cosmetic surgery, thermal cures, mesotherapy, thalassotherapy, homeopathy/alternative medicine, care provided outside the national territory, non-contracted providers.
- **Persons covered (Art. 5-7):** public servants, public/parapublic/private-sector employees, self-employed workers, retirees/pensioners, pupils and students, **economically weak Beninese** (contributions covered by national solidarity), refugees and stateless persons in good standing.
- **Card required (Art. 37):** health insurance card / registration card (except in duly established emergencies).
- **Co-payment (ticket modérateur):** the share of costs remaining payable by the insured; certain vulnerable categories may be exempted (Art. 21).
- **Why this is not a service desk for the user:** RAMU is the framework. **Direct the user to ARCH (below), not to RAMU as such.**
- **Source:** Law 2015-42 (official PDF), Official Journal of the Republic of Benin.

## PROGRAM 1 — HEALTH INSURANCE: ARCH (for the poor) + AMO (mandatory, for all) ⭐

- **Program name:** Mandatory Health Insurance (AMO) — delivered for the poor via the **ARCH** project
- **Managing body:** **ANPS** — National Social Protection Agency (under the Ministry of Social Affairs and Microfinance). Subscription is also possible through an **approved private insurer**.
- **Legal basis:** Law 2015-42 (RAMU framework) + Decree 2023-327 (21 Jun 2023) + Decree 2025-672 (29 Oct 2025, makes it mandatory for all).

### Who it is for (eligibility) — two financing tracks
- **A. Covered by the State (premium financed wholly or partly):**
  - **Extreme poor** → premium **covered 100%** (free care, **no co-payment**). Identified via **PMT (Proxy Means Test)** survey / Single Social Registry (RSU).
  - **Non-extreme poor** → **partial** State coverage.
  - Also State-supported: public agents and their dependents, student grant-holders, public-sector retirees, and very-low-income private-sector retirees.
- **B. Contributory (must pay a premium):**
  - **Private-sector employers must affiliate ALL their employees** (and dependents) by **29 October 2026** — penalty 200,000 FCFA per uncovered employee.
  - Self-employed / informal workers and others not in a State-covered category.

### What is covered (base care package)
- All **general consultations** (general medicine).
- **22 medical conditions and procedures** + the related **biomedical analyses** and **medication**.
  - Documented examples of the 22: **malaria**, **diarrhea with dehydration**, **digestive infections**, **acute respiratory infections**, **urinary infections**, **sepsis**, **bacterial skin infections in children**, **traumas** (limb, pelvic, **cranial**), **simple and complex obstetric fistulas**.
- Hospitalization for common pathologies, **prenatal follow-up**, emergencies **with imaging**, **complex obstetric situations**, severe infections.
- For the State-covered poor under ARCH, coverage is guaranteed for a renewable **3-year** period.

### Cost (contributory track) — base package annual premium
- **Children: 9,000 FCFA / year** (< 1,000 FCFA/month).
- **Adults: 16,000 FCFA / year** (< ~1,340 FCFA/month).
- For employees: the **employer pays at least 80%** of the premium, the employee pays the remaining ~20%.
- **Co-payment (ticket modérateur): 20%** of care costs, paid by the beneficiary — **EXCEPT the extreme poor (exempt, fully free)**.

### Documents required
- **Biometric National Identity Card** issued by **ANIP** (National Agency for the Identification of Persons) — ESSENTIAL / cross-cutting prerequisite.
- For the poor track: having been identified through the social survey / **RSU** (Single Social Registry).

### Exact procedure (steps)
1. Obtain an **ANIP biometric card** first if you don't have one.
2. **If you may be poor/vulnerable:** check that your **commune is covered**, then get **enrolled** during ANPS enrollment campaigns (targeted households are identified via PMT/RSU) → collect your **ARCH biometric card** → present it at a public/contracted health center.
3. **If you are a worker/employer (contributory):** subscribe via the **ANPS** or an **approved private insurer**; employers affiliate their staff and pay ≥80% of the premium.
4. Present the card (except in duly established emergencies) at a contracted facility to access the package.

### Communes covered (poor track, ARCH)
Already covered (≈21, extending to all 77): Abomey-Calavi, Sô-Ava, Dassa-Zoumè, Glazoué, Djougou, Ouaké, Copargo, Cotonou, Avrankou, Ifangni, Aplahoué, Lokossa, Lalo, Kandi, Banikoara, Tchaourou, Ouessè.

### Official contact
- ANPS — Phone: **+229 20 21 34 14** — Email: **masm.anps@gouv.bj**
- Address: Immeuble HOUNDEKON, Sainte Rita district, Cotonou
- Website: **anps.social.gouv.bj** | Ministry: **social.gouv.bj**

### What it does not cover / limits
- The poor track is **not open free enrollment** — you must be **identified as poor via PMT/RSU**.
- Coverage of the poor is not yet generalized to all communes (check your commune).
- Beyond the base package (22 conditions), care may remain payable; **20% co-payment** applies to everyone except the extreme poor.
- ANIP biometric card mandatory (a barrier for the unregistered).
- ⚠️ **Dialysis is NOT free** in Benin (only a Sept 2024 customs/tax exemption on dialysis-kit components reduces cost). **Insulin/diabetes medicines are not free** either — affordability efforts only. Do not tell users these are free.
- **Sources:** gouv.bj (art. 1145, 505, 1536), social.gouv.bj, anps.social.gouv.bj, sgg.gouv.bj (Decree 2023-327), Decree 2025-672 (29 Oct 2025), Cairn/Afrique contemporaine 2025, baobab-courtage.com, lanouvelletribune.info (2024-2025).

## PROGRAM 2 — ARCH: TRAINING COMPONENT (artisans)

- **Name:** ARCH Training Component
- **Body:** ANPS
- **Who it is for:** artisans and vulnerable workers in the trades (agriculture, crafts, art, culture, trade, transport) wishing to strengthen their skills.
- **What it offers:** vocational training / skills development programs (e.g. a training program launched for 4,000 artisans in 2025), a gateway to the **ARCH Credit** (component 3).
- **Documents required:** ANIP biometric card; identification as an ARCH beneficiary.
- **Procedure:** register with the ANPS / local offices during training campaigns in your commune.
- **Contact:** ANPS — +229 20 21 34 14 — masm.anps@gouv.bj — anps.social.gouv.bj
- **Limits:** reserved for identified ARCH beneficiaries; depends on sessions opened by the ANPS.
- **Sources:** anps.social.gouv.bj, La Nouvelle Tribune (March 2025).

## PROGRAM 3 — ARCH: ARCH CREDIT (trained artisans)

- **Name:** ARCH Credit (concessional financing)
- **Body:** ANPS (in connection with the microfinance system)
- **Who it is for:** artisans **who have completed the ARCH training** (component 2), to finance an income-generating activity.
- **What it offers:** financing on favorable (concessional) terms to launch/develop an activity.
- **Documents required:** proof of ARCH training, ANIP biometric card, ID document.
- **Procedure:** complete the training (component 2) → apply for ARCH credit via the ANPS.
- **Contact:** ANPS — +229 20 21 34 14 — anps.social.gouv.bj
- **Limits:** conditional on prior training; does not replace the Alafia Microcredit (a separate program, see Program 5).
- **Sources:** anps.social.gouv.bj.

## PROGRAM 4 — ARCH: RETIREMENT INSURANCE (self-employed / artisans / informal sector)

- **Name:** ARCH Retirement Insurance Component
- **Body:** ANPS (collection supported by the CNSS)
- **Who it is for:** self-employed workers, artisans and **informal-sector** workers (not covered by an employee pension).
- **What it offers:** building a retirement pension for the self-employed.
- **Contribution:** **30,000 FCFA / year**, with a **State contribution of 20%**.
- **Documents required:** ANIP biometric card, ID document, self-employed/artisan status.
- **Procedure:** enroll via the ANPS / partner structures during enrollment campaigns.
- **Contact:** ANPS — +229 20 21 34 14 — anps.social.gouv.bj | CNSS: **+229 90 19 00 00** — cnss.bj
- **Limits:** assumes the ability to contribute annually; progressive rollout.
- **Sources:** CLEISS (social security for the self-employed in Benin), cnss.bj, anps.social.gouv.bj.

## PROGRAM 5 — ALAFIA MICROCREDIT (FNM) — separate from ARCH

- **Name:** Alafia Microcredit (Alafia III phase ongoing: Oct. 2025 → March 2026)
- **Body:** **FNM** — National Microfinance Fund, through partner **SFDs** (Decentralized Financial Systems).
- **Who it is for:** Beninese from vulnerable groups (especially **women**), male and female entrepreneurs across all **77 communes**, with an income-generating activity or a business project.
- **What it offers:** a loan of **30,000, 50,000 or 100,000 FCFA** (100,000 available from the first application since phase 2).
- **Conditions:**
  - Beninese nationality + belonging to vulnerable groups.
  - Having an **income-generating activity** or a project.
  - Forming a **joint-liability group of 3 people**.
  - Holding an ID document (CNI/LEPI/RAVIP).
- **Costs:** interest rate **8% / year**; **400 FCFA** training fee; death insurance **1.2%** of the loan.
- **Procedure:** approach an **FNM partner SFD** in your commune → form the joint-liability group → submit the application → training → disbursement.
- **Contact:** FNM — **fnm.bj** | SFD directory: fnm.bj/repartition-des-sfd-microcredit-alafia | Ministry: social.gouv.bj
- **Limits:** requires a joint-liability group; capped amounts; repayable (≠ a non-repayable transfer). A tool for economic empowerment, **not medical aid**.
- **Sources:** fnm.bj, beninrevele.bj, gouv.bj (art. 913), social.gouv.bj.

## PROGRAM 6 — GBESSOKÉ: Productive Social Safety Nets (cash transfers)

- **Name:** Productive Social Safety Nets Program (PFPSP) "GBESSOKÉ"
- **Body:** Ministry of Social Affairs and Microfinance (implemented by ANPS / community relays).
- **Who it is for:** **households in extreme poverty** (target: 150,000 households, ≈ 1 million people; 85% headed by women in the pilot phase).
- **What it offers:**
  - **Non-repayable monthly cash transfers** for **9 months**.
  - **2 additional payments of 50,000 FCFA** (in the 6th and 9th month) to launch an **Income-Generating Activity (IGA)**.
  - Close productive support (community relays, "Gups").
- **Documents required:** identification as an extreme-poor household (Single Social Registry / survey); SIM card distributed to beneficiaries (mobile payment).
- **Procedure:** households are **identified and targeted** by the social survey (no free application) → enrollment → SIM card reception → mobile transfers.
- **Coverage:** pilot phase in **12 communes** (one per department), expanding.
- **Contact:** Ministry of Social Affairs — **social.gouv.bj** | ANPS — +229 20 21 34 14
- **Limits:** targeting by survey (you do not "apply" to join); pilot phase; tied to a household, not an individual.
- **Sources:** social.gouv.bj, lanationbenin.info, banouto.bj (Sept. 2025).

## PROGRAM 7 — FREE CESAREAN SECTION

- **Name:** National policy of free cesarean section
- **Body:** Ministry of Health (approved public health facilities)
- **Who it is for:** **all women** requiring a cesarean section at an authorized public maternity facility.
- **What it offers:** **free** coverage of the cesarean procedure (in force since 2005).
- **Documents required:** report to the public maternity / hospital; patient's ID document if available (emergencies take precedence).
- **Procedure:** go to a **public health facility** offering maternity services; the free coverage applies on the spot, with no prior registration.
- **Contact:** Ministry of Health — **sante.gouv.bj** (historical alias beninsante.bj) | nearest public health center.
- **Limits:** concerns the cesarean procedure itself; some consumables/related care may remain payable depending on the facility.
- **Sources:** WHO Benin, Cairn/Santé Publique, santetropicale.com.

## PROGRAM 8 — FREE MALARIA TREATMENT (children < 5 & pregnant women)

- **Name:** Free malaria treatment for children under 5 and pregnant women
- **Body:** Ministry of Health / National Malaria Control Program (PNLP)
- **Who it is for:** **children under 5** and **pregnant women** (in force since 2007).
- **What it offers:** **free treatment** of malaria (and associated prevention/management) in public facilities.
- **Documents required:** report to a public health center with the child; health record / birth certificate helpful, but access to care takes precedence.
- **Procedure:** go to the nearest public health center as soon as symptoms appear; request free malaria coverage for under-5 children / pregnant women.
- **Contact:** Ministry of Health — sante.gouv.bj | nearest public health center.
- **Limits:** targeted at under-5s and pregnant women; covers malaria (not all diseases). Malaria remains the leading cause of child mortality.
- **Sources:** WHO Benin, UNICEF Benin, santetropicale.com.

## PROGRAM 9 — FREE ARVs / HIV CARE

- **Name:** Free antiretrovirals (ARVs) and HIV/AIDS care
- **Body:** Ministry of Health / National HIV control program (UN/UNDP support, partners).
- **Who it is for:** **people living with HIV**; includes prevention of mother-to-child transmission (HIV-positive pregnant women, exposed children).
- **What it offers:** **free ARVs** (since 2004), testing, follow-up, prevention of mother-to-child transmission.
- **Documents required:** report to an approved care / testing center; confidentiality guaranteed.
- **Procedure:** get tested at a health center or testing center → if HIV-positive, referral to a care center providing free ARVs.
- **Contact:** Ministry of Health — sante.gouv.bj | ABMS (Benin Association for Social Marketing) — abmsbj.org | local testing center.
- **Limits:** concerns HIV; adherence and regular follow-up are required.
- **Sources:** WHO, UN Benin (joint HIV program), abmsbj.org.

## PROGRAM 10 — FAMILY PLANNING & WOMEN'S CANCER SCREENING (free campaigns)

- **Name:** National campaigns of free family planning + free screening for cervical and breast cancers
- **Body:** Ministry of Health (periodic campaigns)
- **Who it is for:** **women of reproductive age** (contraception); cancer screening for women concerned.
- **What it offers:** free contraceptive methods + free cervical/breast cancer screening during campaigns (e.g. campaign 31 March → 4 April 2026: target 13,200 new contraception users, 156,000 women screened).
- **Documents required:** report to the health center during the campaign; ID document helpful.
- **Procedure:** go to a participating health center during the announced campaign period.
- **Contact:** Ministry of Health — sante.gouv.bj | local public health center.
- **Limits:** ⚠️ **service offered through periodic campaigns**, not continuously year-round — check the dates of the current campaign. Contraceptive prevalence is low (≈16.9%).
- **Sources:** gouv.bj (art. 3479), WHO Afro, beninrevele.com, baatonu.info (April 2026).

## PROGRAM 11 — CNSS: OLD-AGE PENSION (formal sector / employees)

- **Name:** Pensions branch — Old-age pension
- **Body:** CNSS — National Social Security Fund
- **Who it is for:** **salaried workers** affiliated with the CNSS (formal sector) and their dependents.
- **What it offers:** retirement (old-age) pension, family benefits, occupational risks per the Social Security Code.
- **Documents required:** CNSS registration number, ID document, career/contribution records.
- **Procedure:** claim the pension from the CNSS at the required age, based on contributions paid by employer and employee.
- **Contact:** CNSS — **+229 90 19 00 00** — **cnss.bj**
- **Limits:** reserved for employees who have contributed (formal sector); the self-employed/informal fall under the ARCH retirement component (Program 4) or the CMPS.
- **Sources:** cnss.bj, Benin Social Security Code (Droit-Afrique), CLEISS.

## GUARDRAIL REMINDERS (to apply in every AI response)
- Always say "you **could** be eligible", never "you are eligible".
- Always end with "**verify with the official body** before taking any steps".
- Many ARCH/GBESSOKÉ programs work through **survey-based targeting (PMT/RSU)**: the user does not freely "register" → direct them to the ANPS / town hall / social center of their commune.
- The **ANIP biometric card** is a cross-cutting prerequisite — if the user does not have it, the first step is ANIP.
- For **periodic campaigns** (family planning/screening), tell the user to check the current dates.
