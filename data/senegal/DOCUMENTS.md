# REQUIRED DOCUMENTS — SENEGAL
### NAWIRI — Exact pieces to provide for each program (the real friction point)
**Country:** Republic of Senegal
**Last updated:** 15 June 2026
**Purpose:** People miss aid most often because of ONE missing paper. This file lists, per program, the exact documents to bring AND how to obtain the foundational ones when they are missing. Sources at the bottom.

> HOW TO USE THIS (for the AI): when you point a user to a program, tell them the exact documents to prepare. If they lack a foundational piece (birth certificate, CNI, RNU registration), make obtaining that piece the FIRST concrete step.

---

## SECTION A — FOUNDATIONAL / CROSS-CUTTING DOCUMENTS (get these first)

### A1. Birth certificate (extrait d'acte de naissance)
- **Why it matters:** required to prove a child's age (under-5 free care), to apply for the CNI, and for most enrolments.
- **How to get it:** the **civil-status office (état civil) of the town hall** of the place of birth.
- **If never declared / no birth record:** apply for a **jugement supplétif d'acte de naissance** at the competent tribunal — a frequent blocker for rural/informal families; flag it early.
- **Note:** for the CNI, the birth certificate is often required to be **recent (less than 3 months, or less than 12 months for first applications)**.

### A2. CNI — biometric national identity card (CEDEAO standard)
- **Why it matters:** the standard adult ID for CMU mutual enrolment, Plan Sésame (proof of age 60+), and most programs.
- **Who:** any Senegalese from age 5; **mandatory from age 15**.
- **Documents to provide:**
  - **Renewal / already had one:** old CNI **or** a birth certificate (recent), and/or the CEDEAO passport.
  - **First application:** an **original birth certificate (recent)** + an **original certificate of Senegalese nationality** + a **copy of the father's or mother's ID card**. A **nationality certificate** is required if nationality is in doubt.
- **How:** at a **police station / commissariat** (or consulate abroad); applicant must appear in person for both application and collection. Processing ~2 weeks.

### A3. RNU — registration in the National Single Registry (targeting)
- **What it is:** the **Registre National Unique** managed by the **DGPSN**; it is the gateway to the **PNBSF family grant** and **free CMU enrolment for the indigent**.
- **Implication:** you do NOT receive these by filing a folder — households are **identified through an RNU survey / targeting committee**. The actionable step is to get the household **registered/updated at the town hall (mairie) or the departmental social services (Service Départemental de l'Action Sociale)** so it can be considered in the next RNU update (a national RNU update & extension survey ran in 2024–2025).
- **What to have ready:** household identity documents (CNI of the head, birth certificates of children) so the household can be recorded.

## SECTION B — DOCUMENTS PER PROGRAM

### Program 1 — CMU community health mutual
- **To join a mutual:** an **ID document (CNI)**, be **18+**, and **information on your dependents** — then commit to paying the contribution.
- **No prior medical exam**, no minimum-income condition.
- **Contribution:** 7,000 FCFA/person/yr (3,500 if 50%-subsidised; **free for the indigent identified via the RNU** — A3) + a one-time **membership fee** set by each mutual.
- **Where:** the **community health mutual of your commune** (≥1 per commune), or the town hall.

### Program 2 — Free care for children under 5
- **Only proof of the child's age:** the child's **health booklet, birth certificate, vaccination booklet, or any civil-status document** showing age < 5.
- **No prior registration** — present the child at any **public** health post/centre/hospital. If the birth act is missing, get it (A1), but urgent care should not wait.

### Program 3 — Plan Sésame (elderly 60+)
- **An ID document proving age 60+** (CNI). Proof of residence helps.
- Present at a **public** health facility; care under Plan Sésame applies on site.

### Program 4 — Free dialysis
- **A nephrologist's prescription** confirming chronic kidney failure.
- An **ID document**.
- Then **register on the waiting list** of a public dialysis centre (slots are limited).

### Program 5 — Free cesarean section
- **Go to a public maternity/hospital** — emergencies take precedence, no prior registration.
- Bring the woman's **ID document** if available.

### Program 6 — PNBSF family security grant (cash transfer)
- **Be registered in the RNU** (A3) — this is the decisive condition; selection is made from the RNU, not by an open application.
- **Household identification documents** (head of household's CNI, children's birth certificates).
- The 2026 cohort (355,013 beneficiaries) included poor households, **holders of the Equal Opportunity Card** (Program 7), and families of street children — i.e. having the disability card can be a route in.
- **Social conditionalities:** keep children in school, follow health/vaccination check-ups, register births.

### Program 7 — Equal Opportunity Card (persons with disabilities)
- **A certificate of invalidity (certificat médical d'invalidité)** issued by a **doctor approved by the Ministry of Health and Social Action** — the key medical piece.
- An **ID document** and **civil-status documents**.
- **Where to apply:** the **decentralised social action service** of your district (CPRS / SDAS — Service Départemental de l'Action Sociale).
- **Process:** assessment by the **departmental technical commission** → the card is issued by the Ministry in charge of Social Action.

## SECTION C — QUICK DOCUMENT CHECKLIST (for the AI to scan)

| If the user needs… | Make sure they have… | If missing, first step |
|---|---|---|
| CMU health mutual | CNI + dependents info | Get/renew the CNI at a commissariat (birth certificate first if needed) |
| Free under-5 care | Proof of the child's age | Get the birth certificate at the town hall; urgent care still goes ahead |
| Plan Sésame (60+) | ID proving age 60+ | Get/renew the CNI |
| Free dialysis | Nephrologist's prescription | See a nephrologist → register on the centre's waiting list |
| PNBSF cash grant | To be in the RNU | Register the household at the mairie / departmental social services |
| Equal Opportunity Card | Approved doctor's invalidity certificate | Get assessed by an approved doctor → apply at the SDAS/CPRS |
| Anyone with NO birth record | — | **Jugement supplétif d'acte de naissance** at the tribunal (unblocks the CNI and the rest) |
