# REQUIRED DOCUMENTS — BENIN
### NAWIRI — Exact pieces to provide for each program (the real friction point)
**Country:** Republic of Benin
**Last updated:** 15 June 2026
**Purpose:** People miss aid most often because of ONE missing paper. This file lists, per program, the exact documents to bring AND how to obtain the foundational ones when they are missing. Sources at the bottom.

> HOW TO USE THIS (for the AI): when you point a user to a program, tell them the exact documents to prepare. If they lack a foundational piece (ANIP card, secured birth certificate), make obtaining that piece the FIRST concrete step — do not send them to a desk where they will be turned away.

---

## SECTION A — FOUNDATIONAL / CROSS-CUTTING DOCUMENTS (get these first)

These are prerequisites for almost everything. Most program failures happen here.

### A1. NPI / CIP — the personal identification number & certificate (ANIP)
- **What it is:** the **NPI (Numéro Personnel d'Identification)** is the unique ID number assigned in the **RNPP** (national register of physical persons). The **CIP / CNPI (Certificat du Numéro Personnel d'Identification)** is the printable proof of that number.
- **Why it matters:** it is the gateway to the biometric card, AMO/ARCH health insurance, and most administrative steps.
- **How to get it:** online at **eservices.anip.bj** or the **ANIP BJ** mobile app, or at an ANIP office / town hall.
- **Documents to provide:** RAVIP receipt (recensement) **or** a birth certificate; civil-status information.
- **Cost:** the **CNPI is now free**.

### A2. Secured birth certificate (acte de naissance sécurisé — ANIP)
- **What it is:** the modern, secured copy of the birth certificate issued by ANIP (replaces old town-hall copies for many procedures).
- **Why it matters:** required for the biometric card and many enrollments; children's programs need the child's birth certificate.
- **How to get it:** ANIP / town hall of the place of birth; online via eservices.anip.bj where available.
- **If the person was never declared at birth:** they need a **jugement supplétif d'acte de naissance** (a court-issued substitute birth record) from the competent tribunal — this is a common blocker; flag it early.

### A3. Biometric National Identity Card (CNIB — ANIP)
- **What it is:** the official biometric national ID card.
- **Documents to provide (assemble all):**
  1. **RAVIP receipt** (proof of biometric census enrolment),
  2. **CIP / NPI certificate** (A1),
  3. a **"photo bijou"** (compliant ID photo),
  4. a **secured copy of the birth certificate** issued by ANIP (A2),
  5. the **treasury payment receipt (quittance)** for the card-issuance fee.
- **How:** eservices.anip.bj / ANIP BJ app / ANIP office.

### A4. RSU — Single Social Registry (targeting, not a document you "carry")
- **What it is:** the **Registre Social Unique** (WURI/World Bank-supported) used to identify poor households for ARCH free health insurance, GBESSOKÉ, FAABA.
- **Implication:** you do NOT apply with a folder of papers — households are **identified via a PMT survey and community validation** in their commune. The actionable step is to be present/registered when the **ANPS enrolment campaign** passes in your commune, and to hold an ANIP identity so you can be recorded.

## SECTION B — DOCUMENTS PER PROGRAM

### Program 1 — Health Insurance (ARCH for the poor / AMO for all)
- **Poor track (ARCH, free):**
  - **ANIP biometric card** (A3) — essential.
  - Have been **identified as poor via the RSU/PMT survey** (A4) in a covered commune.
  - Then: collect your **ARCH biometric card** at the ANPS enrolment point → present it at a contracted public health centre.
- **Contributory track (AMO — workers/employers, informal):**
  - **ANIP biometric card** (A3).
  - For employees: the **employer affiliates you** (employer pays ≥80%); bring your ID + employment link.
  - For informal/self-employed: subscribe at the **ANPS** or an **approved private insurer**; ID + ability to pay the premium (adults 16,000 / children 9,000 FCFA/yr).

### Program 2 — ARCH Training (artisans)
- **ANIP biometric card** (A3); identification as an **ARCH beneficiary**; register during a training campaign in your commune.

### Program 3 — ARCH Credit
- **Proof of completed ARCH training** (Program 2); **ANIP biometric card** (A3); an ID document. Conditional on having done the training.

### Program 4 — ARCH Retirement (self-employed / informal)
- **ANIP biometric card** (A3); ID document; proof of **self-employed / artisan status**; ability to pay **30,000 FCFA/yr** (State adds 20%).

### Program 5 — Alafia Microcredit (FNM)
- An **ID document: CNI / LEPI / RAVIP**.
- Belong to a **vulnerable group**; have an **income-generating activity or project**.
- **Form a joint-liability group of 3 people** (solidarity group) — this is itself a requirement, not a paper.
- Be ready for fees: 8%/yr interest, **400 FCFA training fee**, 1.2% death insurance.
- Apply at an **FNM partner SFD** in your commune.

### Program 6 — GBESSOKÉ (cash transfers, extreme poor)
- **No application folder** — households are **targeted by the social survey / RSU** (A4).
- Beneficiaries receive a **SIM card** for mobile payments. The step is to be identified during the survey in a covered commune; hold an ANIP identity.

### Program 7 — Free cesarean section
- **Go straight to a public maternity/hospital** — emergencies take precedence, no prior registration.
- Bring if available: the woman's **ID document** and **antenatal/health record**. Lack of papers must not block emergency care.

### Program 8 — Free malaria treatment (under-5 & pregnant women)
- **Go to a public health centre** as soon as symptoms appear; access to care takes precedence.
- Bring if available: the **child's health record / birth certificate** (to prove age < 5), or proof of pregnancy.

### Program 9 — Free ARVs / HIV care
- **No special paperwork to be tested** — go to a health/testing centre; **confidentiality guaranteed**. If positive, you are referred to a care centre providing free ARVs. An ID helps for the file but care is not refused for lack of it.

### Program 10 — Family planning & women's cancer screening (campaigns)
- **Go to a participating health centre during the announced campaign**; an **ID document helps** but is not a strict barrier. ⚠️ check the current campaign dates.

### Program 11 — CNSS old-age pension (formal sector)
- **CNSS registration/insured number**.
- **Family booklet (livret de famille) OR birth certificate OR jugement supplétif** of birth.
- **Career / contribution records**; ID document.
- Eligibility: **age 60** with at least **180 months (15 yrs)** of effective contributions (early claim possible from 55 with a 5%/yr reduction).
- Forms: official "Demande de pension de vieillesse" + "Pièces à fournir" list at **cnss.bj/imprimes-cnss**.

### Program 12 — FAABA / SWEDD (girls 9–15, North)
- **No open application** — girls are **identified via school enrolment + social targeting survey** in Atacora, Donga, Borgou, Alibori.
- Requirements that matter: the girl must be **enrolled in a public primary school / lower-secondary college** and **keep attending** (the transfer is conditional on attendance).

## SECTION C — QUICK DOCUMENT CHECKLIST (for the AI to scan)

| If the user needs… | Make sure they have… | If missing, first step |
|---|---|---|
| Any ARCH/AMO health insurance | ANIP biometric card (A3) | Get NPI/CIP → secured birth certificate → biometric card via eservices.anip.bj |
| Free insurance for the poor (ARCH) | To be in the RSU survey (A4) | Be present for the ANPS enrolment campaign in your commune |
| A child < 5 treated (malaria) | Child's birth certificate/health record | Go to the public centre anyway — care takes precedence; regularise the act later |
| Cesarean / childbirth | Nothing mandatory upfront | Go to a public maternity immediately (emergency) |
| Microcredit (Alafia) | CNI/LEPI/RAVIP + a group of 3 | Form the joint-liability group; visit an FNM partner SFD |
| CNSS pension | Insured number + birth proof + career record | Request records at CNSS; get a jugement supplétif if no birth act |
| Anyone with NO birth record at all | — | **Jugement supplétif d'acte de naissance** at the tribunal (unblocks everything) |
